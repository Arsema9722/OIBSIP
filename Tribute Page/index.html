Abebe Bikila

The Barefoot Olympic Champion

1932 – 1973

Biography

Abebe Bikila was born on August 7, 1932, in Jato, Ethiopia. He rose to international fame at the 1960 Rome Olympic Games when he won the marathon running barefoot, becoming the first Black African athlete to win an Olympic gold medal. His remarkable victory brought global attention to Ethiopia and marked a turning point for African athletes in long-distance running.

Bikila made history again at the 1964 Tokyo Olympic Games by winning a second consecutive Olympic marathon title, becoming the first runner ever to successfully defend an Olympic marathon gold medal. His determination, humility, and groundbreaking achievements made him a national hero and an inspiration around the world. He passed away on October 25, 1973, but his legacy continues to inspire generations of athletes.
